09 · REGULATORY & RISK

Structural advantages, managed risks.

ADVANTAGE
GLP-1s are not controlled substances.
Semaglutide and tirzepatide are not DEA-scheduled. Our core prescribing model does not trigger Ryan Haight Act restrictions — a structural advantage over controlled-substance telehealth peers.
TRANSPARENCY
503B compounding — transparent model.
We source exclusively from FDA-registered 503B outsourcing facilities under CGMP. Compounded semaglutide is NOT FDA-approved. Every patient receives plain-English disclosure and e-signs informed consent. No misleading equivalency claims.
KEY RISK
FDA shortage list — key business risk.
Semaglutide's shortage-list status legally enables 503B compounding. If removed, compounding at scale ends. Mitigation: tirzepatide pathway, branded fallback, multi-pharmacy redundancy, member communication plan.
STRUCTURE
CPOM compliance — MSO structure.
Physician-owned Professional Corporation holds clinical licenses; Sculpted provides admin + technology services under an MSA. Standard, defensible structure for modern telehealth.
LICENSING
Multi-state licensing strategy.
30+ states at launch, all 50 by month 12. 50-state telehealth prescribing matrix maintained by counsel, updated quarterly. States with in-person requirements addressed with modified protocols.
ADVERTISING
FTC and advertising compliance.
Every testimonial complies with FTC Endorsement Guidelines. "Results not typical; individual results vary" on all ads. No unsubstantiated efficacy claims. All creative reviewed by compliance before launch.
